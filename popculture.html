In popular culture

The phrase "the greatest thing since sliced bread" is a common hyperbole used to praise an invention or development. A writer for The Kansas City Star wrote that "the phrase is the ultimate depiction of innovative achievement and American know-how."

In 1940, a package of bread consisting of two wrapped half-loaves was advertised as the "greatest convenience since sliced bread".

Did you know?

Within five years of the invention of the machine for making sliced bread, 80% of bread sold in the USA was sliced.
